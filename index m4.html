Overview

Robert Nesta Marley OM (6 February 1945 - 11 May 1981) was a Jamaican singer, musician, and songwriter. Considered one of the pioneers of reggae, his musical career was marked by fusing elements of reggae, ska, and rocksteady, as well as his distinctive vocal and songwriting style.[2][3] Marley's contributions to music increased the visibility of Jamaican music worldwide, and made him a global figure in popular culture.

Awards

In 1976, Bob Marley was named Band of the Year by Rolling Stone. In June 1978, he was awarded the Peace Medal of the Third World from the United Nations. In February 1981, he received Jamaica's third highest honor, the Jamaican Order of Merit. In March 1994, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. In 1999, his album Exodus was named Album of the Century by Time Magazine. In February 2001, he was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame and received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award. In 2004, Rolling Stone ranked him No.11 on their list of the 100 Greatest Artists of All Time. His song "One Love" was named the song of the millennium by BBC, and he was voted as one of the greatest lyricists of all time in a BBC poll. In 2006, a blue plaque was unveiled at his first UK residence in Ridgmount Gardens, London, dedicated to him by the Nubian Jak community trust and supported by Her Majesty's Foreign Office. In 2010, his album Catch a Fire was inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame in the Reggae Album category.
